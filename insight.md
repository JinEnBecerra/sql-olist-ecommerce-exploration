# Business Insights  
This document summarizes key findings from the three dashboards: Business Performance, Delivery & Customer Experience, and Revenue Drivers.

---

## 1. Revenue Growth Driven by Order Volume  
Revenue increased steadily from 2016 to 2018, with a peak in November 2017. At the same time, Average Order Value (AOV) stayed relatively stable, around R$155–R$170, indicating that revenue growth came mainly from more orders, not higher spending per order.

This means scaling revenue requires growing the customer base and order frequency — not simply raising prices or pushing higher-value products.

---

## 2. Early Fluctuations Due to Low Order Volume  
In the early stage of the platform, both AOV and cancellation rate showed noticeable spikes. For example, there was an AOV anomaly in November 2016 and a spike in cancellation rate, both linked to very low order volume. As order volume increased, these metrics became more stable.

These early anomalies should be excluded from trend analysis to avoid misleading conclusions about platform performance.

---

## 3. Delivery Performance Improved Over Time  
Delivery time was higher in the early stage, then gradually decreased and stabilized. Late delivery rates followed a similar pattern, with more variability early on and more consistency later, suggesting that logistics operations improved as the platform grew.

Since delivery time directly correlates with review scores, continued investment in logistics efficiency is likely to sustain customer satisfaction as the platform scales.

---

## 4. Customer Satisfaction Remained Stable  
Review scores stayed consistent over time, around 4.0–4.2. Even when delivery performance fluctuated in the early stage, customer satisfaction did not drop significantly, indicating that the overall customer experience remained strong.

However, this aggregate stability masks category-level variation — some high-selling categories consistently underperform on ratings, which may not yet be visible in overall scores.

---

## 5. Revenue is Concentrated  
Revenue is not evenly distributed:
- A few states (especially SP) contribute a large share of revenue
- A small number of product categories generate most sales
- Top sellers account for a significant portion of revenue

This concentration creates both opportunity and risk: geographic expansion beyond SP and seller base diversification could unlock significant revenue growth, while over-reliance on a few contributors increases vulnerability to disruption.

---

## 6. High Sales with Low Ratings  
Some product categories have relatively high sales but low average ratings (around 2.0–3.0), indicating that while these products sell well, they are not meeting customer expectations.

If left unaddressed, these categories risk becoming churn drivers — customers who purchase once and do not return due to unmet expectations.

---

## 7. Overall Summary  
The platform demonstrates strong growth fundamentals, but two structural risks require attention: revenue concentration among a small number of states, categories, and sellers; and persistent quality gaps in high-volume categories.

Addressing seller quality in low-rated categories and investing in geographic expansion represent the clearest paths to sustainable long-term growth.
