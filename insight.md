# Business Insights  

This document summarizes key findings from the three dashboards: Business Performance, Delivery & Customer Experience, and Revenue Drivers.

---

## 1. Revenue Growth Driven by Order Volume  

Revenue increased steadily from 2016 to 2018, with a peak in November 2017.  

At the same time, Average Order Value (AOV) stayed relatively stable, around R$155–R$170.  

This suggests that revenue growth came mainly from more orders, not higher spending per order.

---

## 2. Early Fluctuations Due to Low Order Volume  

In the early stage of the platform, both AOV and cancellation rate showed noticeable spikes.  

For example, there was an AOV anomaly in November 2016 and a spike in cancellation rate, both linked to very low order volume.  

As order volume increased, these metrics became more stable.

---

## 3. Delivery Performance Improved Over Time  

Delivery time was higher in the early stage, then gradually decreased and stabilized.  

Late delivery rates followed a similar pattern, with more variability early on and more consistency later.  

This suggests that logistics operations improved as the platform grew.

---

## 4. Customer Satisfaction Remained Stable  

Review scores stayed consistent over time, around 4.0–4.2.  

Even when delivery performance fluctuated in the early stage, customer satisfaction did not drop significantly.  

This indicates that the overall customer experience remained strong.

---

## 5. Revenue is Concentrated  

Revenue is not evenly distributed.  

- A few states (especially SP) contribute a large share of revenue  
- A small number of product categories generate most sales  
- Top sellers account for a significant portion of revenue  

This shows a clear concentration in key areas.

---

## 6. High Sales with Low Ratings  

Some product categories have relatively high sales but low average ratings (around 2.0–3.0).  

This may indicate that while these products sell well, they are not meeting customer expectations.

---

## 7. Overall Summary  

Overall, the business shows steady growth and improving operations.  

However, there are two potential concerns:  
- Heavy reliance on a small number of contributors  
- Low-rated categories that still generate strong sales  

These areas may impact long-term performance if not addressed.
